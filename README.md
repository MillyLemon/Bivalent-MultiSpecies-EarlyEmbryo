# Bivalent-MultiSpecies-EarlyEmbryo
Code &amp; analysis pipeline for master's thesis on bivalent domains and H3K27me3 in early mammalian embryos. Integrates ChIP-seq and RNA-seq data from mouse, human, rat, bovine and porcine to explore cross-species distribution patterns of these chromatin marks.

# Bivalent domains and H3K27me3 in Early Embryonic Development Across Multiple Species

[![License: MIT](https://img.shields.io/badge/License-MIT-yellow.svg)](https://opensource.org/licenses/MIT)

## Overview

This repository contains the complete analysis pipeline and code for the master's thesis:

**"Distribution patterns of Bivalent domains and H3K27me3 during early embryonic development across multiple species"**

*Li Xuemei, Binzhou Medical University, 2026*

## Key Findings

1. **Bivalent domains are conserved across species**: H3K4me3/H3K27me3 co-marked bivalent domains were identified in early embryos of all five species (mouse, human, rat, bovine, porcine), with their target genes showing intermediate expression levels between H3K4me3-only and H3K27me3-only genes.

2. **H3K27me3 exhibits non-canonical narrow distribution**: In addition to the classical broad domains, H3K27me3 forms narrow peaks at promoter and TSS regions in vivo, and some narrow-peak genes remain transcriptionally active, challenging the traditional view that H3K27me3 is always repressive.

3. **Candidate transcription factors identified**: ZNF135, ZBTB18, NR4A2, and PRDM1 were identified as potential regulators associated with narrow H3K27me3 patterns through motif enrichment analysis and integrated regulatory network construction.
